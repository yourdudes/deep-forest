Trade FAQ

Questions buyers ask us

Straight answers on terms, quality and process. Don't see your question? Our trade desk is one message away.

Which Incoterms do you trade on?+

As standard we quote FOB, CFR and CIF under Incoterms® 2020. Other terms such as FCA, CPT and DAP are available by agreement. The applicable Incoterm, named port and rule version are always stated in the contract.

What is the minimum order quantity?+

It depends on the commodity. Containerised products can start from a single full container load, while bulk commodities are typically traded in vessel parcels. Tell us the grade and destination and we'll confirm the workable MOQ.

How is quality verified?+

Quality and quantity are verified by recognised independent inspection agencies before shipment, with sampling and assays per the contract specification. Quality clauses and any rejection or price-adjustment mechanisms are agreed in writing in advance.

What payment terms do you accept?+

We commonly trade against irrevocable letters of credit at sight, confirmed LCs (DLC), standby LCs (SBLC) and documentary collections (CAD/DP). Exact terms are confirmed per transaction based on commodity, volume and counterparty.

How long does delivery take?+

As a guide, expect 2–6 weeks from a confirmed contract and payment instrument to shipment, depending on origin, vessel availability and routing. We provide an indicative shipment window with every offer and keep you updated through to discharge.

Can you handle compliance and documentation?+

Yes. We support KYC and sanctions screening and prepare the full set of shipping and trade documents — commercial invoice, packing list, bill of lading, certificates of origin and inspection, and others your market requires. We work to align each shipment with applicable import, export and safety requirements.
